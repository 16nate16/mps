Caffeine

A report published in Sports Medicine refers to caffeine as a “powerful ergogenic aid,” and mentions that athletes can “train at a greater power output and/or train longer” after caffeine consumption. Caffeine has been proven to boost energy and mental focus, boost your metabolism, improve endurance by reducing your body’s consumption of glycogen (the stored energy consumed during exercise), and reduce the build up of lactic acid (lactic acid depletes glycogen). Caffeine, though beneficial in many regards does lose its effectiveness with sustained consumption. It is recommended that those who take caffeine reduce usage for set periods of time (roughly one week) to allow the body to re-acclimate to the lack of stimulant. Note that caffeine is harmful if consumed in excess. Please consult a medical professional if you have any heart related conditions or if you are pregnant or nursing.

Beta-Alanine

Beta Alanine has been proven to increase the body’s concentration of a Carnosine, which is a natural buffer that decreases the concentration of H+. Increased levels of H+ cause your body’s PH to drop during sustained, high intensity training, diminishing your body's strength and endurance. Therefore, a higher Carnosine concentration allows you to better sustain strength and endurance during your workouts.

L-Arginine

Arginine is an amino acid essential in the body’s production of protein. L-Arginine is also the amino acid broken down in the body’s production of Nitric Oxide. Nitric Oxide improves blood flow, and increases the release of the hormone adrenaline. That being said, L-Arginine also has also been found to boost the immune system and help prevent inflammation.

Creatine Monohydrate

Creatine monohydrate is produced in the body from the amino acids arginine, glycine, and methionine. Creatine is found in skeletal muscle in the form of creatine phosphate. Its main mode of action is to provide an inorganic phosphate to re-synthesize ATP during high intensity activities, typically lasting 10 seconds or less (i.e. sprinting, high intensity weight lifting). In other words, it aids in the body’s production of energy.

Branch Chain Amino Acids

It's well established that branched chain amino acids (particularly leucine) stimulate protein synthesis, and might do so to a greater extent than a normal protein on its own. BCAAs also increase synthesis of the cellular machinery responsible for carrying out the process of protein synthesis. Thus, BCAAs not only increase the rate of protein synthesis, but they also increase the cell's CAPACITY for protein synthesis. BCAAs also work in your favor by reducing the rate of protein breakdown. They do this (primarily) by decreasing the activity of the components of the protein breakdown pathway, and also by decreasing the expression of several complexes involved in protein breakdown.

N-Acetyl L-Tyrosine

This amino acid is critical for a number of different bodily functions such as cognitive processes, building muscles, and cell growth and recovery. Taking N-Acetyl L-Tyrosine can support peak perfor- mance from both your brain and body by alleviating stress, stimulating your mind and improving neural communication. It is one of the cognitive enhancement supplements that can promote better balance in the neurotransmitter levels in your brain. L-Glutamine

During an intense workout the body is stressed and your muscles and tendons require more gluta- mine than the amount supplied by a normal diet. After an intense workout the levels of cellular gluta- mine can drop by 50%! This ‘muscle wasting state’ is a gateway for the body to use your muscle for energy rather than carbohydrates. Glutamine can prevent this from happening. Supplementing with L-glutamine allows your muscles to fight and push a bit further, which boosts strength and it also has an important role in repairing the skeletal muscles.
